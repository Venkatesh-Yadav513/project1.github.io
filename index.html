HISTORICAL PLACES OF INDIA



Taj Mahal

 The Taj Mahal is a white tomb built in the 17th century by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan in memory of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The building is in the city of Agra, Uttar Pradesh. Widely thought as one of the most beautiful buildings in the world, it is one of India's biggest tourist attractions.

India Gate

The India Gate is located at the heart of India’s capital city, New Delhi. About 2.3 km from the Rashtrapati Bhavan, it is located on the eastern extremity of the ceremonial boulevard, Rajpath. India Gate is a war memorial dedicated to honor the soldiers of the Undivided Indian Army who died during World War I between 1914 and 1921.The foundation of this war memorial was placed in the year 1921 by the visiting Duke of Connaught. It took around 10 years for the construction of Dilli India Gate. It was inaugurated in February 1931 by Viceroy Lord Irwin.

Gate Of India

Gateway of India Mumbai is an ancient tourist destination in Mumbai. The Gateway of India, also known as the gateway to India, is an important monument built in the 20th century. Gateway of India is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Mumbai. Information about Gateway of India Mumbai.The main objective behind the construction of the Gateway of India was to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to Bombay (Mumbai). In March 1911, Sir George Sydenham Clarke, who was then the Governor of Bombay, laid down the monument's first foundation.

Red Fort

The Red Fort (Lal Qila) is a monument built in 1638 that rises 33 meters (108 ft) above Old Delhi. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan. The fort is located in what is now the centre of Delhi, India.Red fort was made up of lime stones and when the white stone started chipping off, the Britishers painted it with red paint. 4. Named for its Walls The red fort was originally named by the Britishers after its red walls (which were by the way constructed to provide maximum security to the royals.) The natives translated it to “Lal Quila”.

Gol Gumbaz

Constructed by Yaqut of Dabul, Gol Gumbaz exhibits the Indo-Islamic style of architecture. The dome of Gol Gumbaz is one of the largest domes ever built in the history, with a diameter measuring up to 144 feet. The humongous dome is supported by eight intersecting arches.Mohammed Adil Shah, the 7th ruler of Adil Shahi Dynasty ordered to build this tomb before his death.
